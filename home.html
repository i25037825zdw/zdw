The Legendary Li River

Stretching 83 kilometers from Guilin to Yangshuo, the Li River is like a jade ribbon winding through towering karst peaks. As you cruise along its waters, you will be mesmerized by the ever-changing scenery: dramatic limestone formations reflected perfectly in the crystal-clear water, bamboo rafts gliding peacefully, cormorant fishermen casting their nets at sunset, and idyllic villages nestled along the riverbanks. It is no wonder that the Li River is celebrated as one of the most beautiful river journeys in the world.

Reed Flute Cave

A spectacular limestone cave with colorful lighting illuminating stunning stalactites, stalagmites, and rock formations. Known as the "Palace of Natural Art," this cave has been a popular attraction for over 1,200 years.

Zhenpiyan Heritage Site

An important archaeological site that reveals 10,000 years of human history. Discover the origins of pottery making and the remarkable intelligence of ancient civilizations that once thrived in this region.

A Taste of Guilin

Guilin's culinary scene is a feast for the senses. The city is famous for its Rice Noodles, a beloved dish made from carefully selected early indica rice served with rich broth and toppings like fried peanuts, pickled beans, and fresh herbs. Beyond noodles, you can savor the Three Treasures of Guilin including Fermented Tofu and Sanhua Wine, or try unique local specialties like Stuffed River Snails and Gongcheng Oil Tea. Every bite tells a story of this region's rich heritage.

Guilin Fermented Tofu

One of the Three Treasures of Guilin, this fermented tofu is known for its thin skin, tender texture, and exquisite flavor. Available in both five-spice and chili varieties, it is a perfect souvenir.
